Iterating on the Player Animations

Project Blue 3/23/2020

Review Work

Giving Feedback & Critique:

Over the past 3 weeks I have made the transition from being the Project Lead to being the art director. This change was mainly made as I was doing a lot of animation work and teaching animation to a lot of members, being asked to review art work, being asked to implement art work on the sprites, and this took up a large portion of my time, and as an art student, I found it to be more enjoyable work.

After this change was determined among the leads, I began to give the artists feedback on their in progress works in the art channels. Here, I was giving feedback about the Crystal caves boss animation.

Here I can be seen giving feedback to Naveen about his teleport recharge item in hopes of refining the design and making it more cohesive with the rest of the game's artwork

Following my public feedback, Naveen messaged me a few more times asking for further advice as he began to iterate on the work

Here I'm giving advice about one of the artist's enemy animations, in hopes that they could make the walk cycle a bit more natural.

Here Naveen reached out to me asking for more feedback on one of his enemy sprite, and did again a few days later.

Next up, I reworked the entire animator and events that triggered the animations on the player to use bools rather than triggers in an attempt to cut back on the various bugs that we were having with the animator setup

Here is the projectile throw animation I made

And here is how I setup the attack animator. It's on another animator layer with a single blendtree triggered by entering from any state based on an animation trigger, and another state that it starts at by default with no motion. The layer is weighted at 1 and overrides any motion the upper body was doing for the lower layer animation in order to let the player attack while in any state.
